Ecosystem Services

Pollination

Up to two-thirds of all crops require some degree of animal pollination to reach their maximum yields, and natural habitat around farmlands can support healthy populations of wild pollinators by providing them with foraging and nesting resources.

→ To Pollination Modeling

Water Quality Regulation

Fertilizers like nitrogen are a major source pollution to freshwater systems and drinking water. However, some of it may be retained by healthy ecosystems, and this nitrogen retention regulating drinking water quality is one of nature’s contributions to people.

→ To Water Quality Regulation Modeling

Coastal Risk Mitigation

Coastal habitats such as coral reefs, mangroves, salt marsh, or sea grass, attenuate waves and protect the shorelines from the impacts of storms, such as floods and erosion.

→ To Coastal Risk Mitigation Modeling

How to read these maps?

    This map highlight where people will be most at risk in the future. By using the control panel different services and scenarios can be explored.

  • The Deficit-mode displays where people are most in need of nature’s contribution. The People Impacted-mode displays how big the impacted population are and the Nature’s contribution-mode shows how much nature helps to reduce risks in different parts of the world.

  • Choose which service to explore by selecting Coastal risk, Water quality or Pollination with the radio buttons.

  • Choose which scenario to explore by selecting Green Growth, Regional Rivalry or Fossil Fuels with the radio buttons.

  • For more detailed data and the possibility to compare services and scenarios, select a country by clicking on it.
