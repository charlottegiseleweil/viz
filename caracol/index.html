About this Figure

The first two maps represent a palette of options for future development. The "more conservative" options allow for an increase in tourism, selective site development, improved agricultural practices and continued sustainable timber, with a focus on minimal expansion into natural areas, and minimal impact on natural resources.
"Less conservative" options allow for a greater increase in tourism, more site development, expansion of agriculture into natural areas and continued sustainable timber. These generally lead to a greater impact on natural resources.
For each set of development options, a synopsis of projected changes in ecosystem services, revenue and community impact are provided. The first graph shows a simple, relative comparison of how these are likely to change under each set of development options. The second set of graphs provide more detail, with percent change given for each environmental, revenue and community impact.

Welcome!

This viewer shows different scenarios for development in the CMCC Region in Belize.

Mapping Elements

To visualize the information we have an interactive map where you can turn layers on and off to explore different factors. To the right of the page you have the main selection panel where you have buttons that allow for greater interaction with the map, as well as checkboxes that can be clicked to turn layers on and off. Make sure to click and hover over icons and layers for additional information!

An interactive legend on the bottom left corner of the map will respond to the layers you are displaying on the map.

The Buttons


Scenarios Button

This button shows a figure comparing different future scenarios for development in this region and the impact on ecosystem services.

Data Download Button

This button will display links that allow you to download the data files present in this viewer. Click the button to turn the data links on and off.

About Button

This button will take you to a panel where you can find and download additional resources about this project, including reports, method descriptions, and links to additional information.

The Scenario Comparison Panel


The top row in the static figure has land cover maps that show models of possible expansion of the four main development areas we observed for this study: Agriculture, Mining, Tourism, and Timber. The second and third row look at metrics to evaluate how different ecoystem services will change under these development conditions. Below the graph you will find tools to expand the graph for further inspection and for download, as well as some description of some of the services evaluated.
