About The Sustainable Development Plan for the CMCC Region


Pending plans to improve the road network from Georgeville to Caracol have raised concerns that unplanned development and improved access could lead to degradation of both natural and cultural assets, if done without proactive planning. Improvements to the Caracol road are expected to increase visitors to the region, which in turn can impact water supplies, waste disposal, and security, while potentially bringing jobs and economic growth to the area.
State-of-the-art ecosystem services mapping and analysis was done with a strong focus on stakeholder interests, values, and knowledge. The goal is to create an ecosystem-based plan that maintains the long-term economic prosperity of the region and balances resource use with environmental protection and local livelihoods.
This web-based viewer provides an overview of the stakeholder-driven data used to guide this work, as well as primary results. Maps are provided showing areas where development is more or less likely to impact cultural sites, water supplies and other community resources. Two planning options are also provided, one that takes a more conservative approach to development, the other taking a less conservative approach. For each planning option, a synopsis is provided of projected environmental, economic and social impacts. For more detailed information about this analysis, please see the Sustainable Development Plan report.

Method

For the purposes of scientific analysis of ecosystem services, the total study area includes the protected areas of the CMCC complex, the Vaca Forest Reserve and Elijio Panti National Park, as well as the buffer areas and watershed area that drains to the city of Belmopan. While the SDP will be focused on the CMCC, the larger area is critical for analyzing the flow of services and impacts in and around the CMCC. The development of an SDP for the CMCC region, one that is informed by an assessment of ecosystem services, development options, and trade-offs, encompasses the following steps:
  1. Identify key ecosystem services and benefits
  2. Identify development pressures
  3. Ecosystem service modeling
    • Develop and calibrate baseline models
    • Analyze scenarios to identify hotspots of potential impact
  4. Economic modeling
  5. Identify sustainable development options
  6. Analyze trade-offs
  7. Governance
  8. Develop SDP

How to Use the Viewer

Click on the checkboxes on the panel on the right to turn data layers on and off. If you click on some of the icons you can learn more information about the data. You can also press the data download button for links to be able to access files displayed. For more information click on the 'Start Tutorial' button at the top of this panel.
